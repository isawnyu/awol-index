Aegean Archaeology: Studies and Monographs in Mediterranean Archaeology and Civilization. Aegean Archaeology is published annually by the Institute of Archaeology and Ethnology of the Polish Academy of Sciences in Warsaw, and co-edited in the Department of Classics at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. The journal encourages contributions that concern the Aegean world - the cultures and societies that comprised the civilizations of the Aegean basin and its bordering regions, principally the Greek and Anatolian Aegean in the Neolithic, Bronze Age, Early Iron Age and Archaic periods. A single article from each of the six most recent volumes is available in open access": VOLUME 10, 2009-2010(2013), 120p. Y. Papadatos, and Ch.
