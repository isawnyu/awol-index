Trafficking Culture: Researching the Global Traffic in Looted Cultural Objects

description
Trafficking Culture: Researching the Global Traffic in Looted Cultural Objects. <blockquote class="tr_bq">Trafficking Culture aims to produce an evidence-based picture of the contemporary global trade in looted cultural objects. This research programme is based at the University of Glasgow and is funded by the European Research Council. On this website, you can keep up to date with the research as it progresses. The <a href="http://traffickingculture. org/projects/" title="Projects">Projects</a> page outlines the research activities which make up the overall research programme, and links to summaries of work to be undertaken in each research strand. The <a href="http://traffickingculture. org/encyclopedia/" title="Encyclopedia">Encyclopedia</a> is a constantly evolving resource which presents introductory materials on the research topic. We encourage you to start here if you are looking for a short <a href="http://traffickingculture. org/encyclopedia/case-studies/" title="Case Studies">case study</a> of a famously looted artefact, a critical analysis of a relevant <a href="http://traffickingculture. org/encyclopedia/law/" title="Law">law</a>, a selection of <a href="http://traffickingculture. org/encyclopedia/theory-and-method/" title="Theory and Method">methodological orientations</a> for conducting research on the topic, or a brief introduction to criminological or social <a href="http://traffickingculture. org/encyclopedia/theory-and-method/" title="Theory and Method">theory</a> which may help us understand and engage with the issues. The <a href="http://traffickingculture. org/publications/" title="Publications">Publications</a> tab will take you to our research output past and present. We very much welcome communication from researchers and others interested in the topic, including prospective PhD students looking for supervision.
