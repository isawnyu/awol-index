Obsidian Trade in the Near East, 14,000 to 6500 BC

Obsidian Trade in the Near East, 14,000 to 6500 BC (2005). Obsidian, a black volcanic glass, was first recognized by Colin Renfrew and his colleagues J. E. Dixon and J. R. Cann in the 1960s as a uniquely sensitive indicator of prehistoric trade, both because of the great desirability of this material before the use of metals, and also because the trace-elements it contains are usually diagnostic of individual sources.
