The AMPHORAS Project. <blockquote class="tr_bq">This site contains information on plain, unglazed, ceramic storage containers, with two handles, mostly pointed at the bottom, used to carry wine, oil, fish, and other commodities around the ancient Mediterranean. <a href="http://projects. chass. utoronto. ca/amphoras/blurb. htm">AMPHORAS</a> is making available part of the archive collected by Virginia R. Grace at the excavations of the Agora at Athens, as well as some <a href="http://projects. chass. utoronto. ca/amphoras/add-mat. htm">additional materials</a>. Included are: <ul><li>a <a href="http://projects. chass. utoronto. ca/amphoras/bib/amph-bib. htm">bibliography</a> of scholarly work on finding, identifying, and studying Greek and Roman amphoras and the trade they carried </li><li><a href="http://projects. chass. utoronto. ca/amphoras/refs/refs. htm">passages</a> in ancient Greek literature on the use of amphoras (quoted in English). </li><li><a href="http://projects. chass. utoronto. ca/amphoras/trans/tran. htm">translations</a> into English of works (or parts of works) published in Russian on amphoras </li><li>links to other <a href="http://projects. chass. utoronto. ca/amphoras/www-amph. htm#beg">Web sites</a> with amphora information and/or images (excavations, wrecks, etc) and other <a href="http://projects. chass. utoronto. ca/amphoras/bib/amph-bib. htm#oth">sources of bibliography</a> </li><li>searches of the bibliography files and the text of other files.
